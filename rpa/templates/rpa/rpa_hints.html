{% translate "Hints for successfully completing a record of processing activities" %}

{% blocktranslate %} By filling out the set of forms in the RPA generator, you will create a record of processing activities (RPA) for the processing of personal data in the context of your research project, administrative activity, or other intent. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} If you do not process personal data, because the data you work with is not personal data per se or you work with anonymised data only, you do not need to create a record of processing activities. Anonymised data is data that does not allow any conclusions about an individual natural person (anymore). That means that the data does neither contain "obvious" personal data, such as a person's name or Email address, nor that the data in its entirety allows to conclude a natural person's identity. If in doubt, ask the DPO. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} In addition to the legal obligation under Art. 30 of the General Data Protection Regulation (you must maintain a record of processing activities and present it to the supervisory authority upon request), your RPA also serves to your own orientation in handling personal data. {% endblocktranslate %}

  • {% blocktranslate %} Complete and amend the information in the subsequent set of forms as appropriate for your processing activity. The forms have built-in logic, that tries to validate the structure of the information you enter as much as possible. It will not prevent you from entering false or incomplete information, though. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Contact the DPO if you are unsure about any information. You may want to read the answers to frequently asked questions ("FAQ") beforehand at {% endblocktranslate %}
    {{ settings.DPO_WEB }}
    {% blocktranslate %} Suggestions for improvements (both to the "FAQ" and to the present interactive RPA generator) are welcome. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Keep in mind that your RPA will be read by the DPO and may be read by someone at the competent supervisory authority. These readers are most likely not familiar with your processing activity. Some form steps have free-text fields, that allow to add individual comments and descriptions - use these to make your processing activity more transparent, but stay concise. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} More extensive information (contracts, concepts, forms for declarations of consent, etc.) can be attached as an annex and maintained in the list of annexes of the RPA. Annexes can be listed in this system, but the corresponding documents need to be added manually to the final version of your RPA. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} The RPA generator considers all information about processing activities covered by the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). In most cases, not all information is required. Therefore, the system will allow you to leave fields blank. Please be aware, that, depending on your actual processing activity, these fields or options may indeed be required. Example: The information about whether there is a data processor assigned, is by default "Unknown". If you have a data processor, change this to "Yes" and fill out the required information. If you don't have a data processor, change this to "No" and leave the other fields untouched. The same is true for a privacy impact assessment, joint controllers, etc. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} Things you should think about before you start with your RPA: {% endblocktranslate %}
    • {% blocktranslate %} A short name of your own choice for your research project, administrative activity, etc. {% endblocktranslate %}
    • {% blocktranslate with controller=settings.CONTROLLER_NAME %} Whether there is a joint controller (a joint controller is a person or entity outside {{ controller }}, who decides together with you on means and purposes of the processing of the personal data in question). There should be a contract regarding joint controllership (add to annex). Typical example: Joint research projects. {% endblocktranslate %}
    • {% blocktranslate with controller=settings.CONTROLLER_NAME %} Whether there is a data processor (a data processor is a person or entity outside {{ controller }}, who is processing the personal data in question according to your instructions). A data processing agreement (DPA) is mandatory in this case (add to annex). Typical examples include hosting, translation/transliteration services and SaaS providers (surveys, collaboration, storage, database applications, etc.). {% endblocktranslate %}
    • {% blocktranslate %} Whether you plan to transfer personal data to a third country (which is any country outside the ambit of the GDPR) or international organisation. If yes, choose "Yes" and provide the information required. {% endblocktranslate %}
    • {% blocktranslate with controller=settings.CONTROLLER_NAME %} Whether your processing activity requires a privacy impact assessment (PIA, or DPIA for "data protection impact assessment", which is the term used in the English translation of the GDPR). The GDPR on PIAs: "Where a type of processing in particular using new technologies, and taking into account the nature, scope, context and purposes of the processing, is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the controller [you] shall, prior to the processing, carry out an assessment of the impact of the envisaged processing operations on the protection of personal data." Privacy impact assessments are relatively rare (at least at {{ controller }}) and complex. Please contact the DPO for details before you attempt to do a PIA. {% endblocktranslate %}
    • {% blocktranslate %} It may be helpful to think about your processing activity as a flow of data: Where does the data originate (e.g. a survey or the campus management system)? Where does it eventually go to (e.g. a transfer to some other entity or simply erasure/anonymisation)? What happens in between (e.g. processing by a data processor)? What measures do you take to ensure data confidentiality and integrity? {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate with controller=settings.CONTROLLER_NAME supauth_name=settings.SUPAUTH_NAME supauth_city=settings.SUPAUTH_CITY %} The competent supervisory authority for compliance with data protection regulations at {{ controller }} is {{ supauth_name }}, located in {{ supauth_city }}. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} In the event of a loss of data in the sense that personal data has become known to unauthorized third parties (or the possibility thereof, e.g. in case of a lost or stolen laptop), the DPO or the supervisory authority must be informed immediately. {% endblocktranslate %}
  • {% blocktranslate %} If you would like to read the original text of the General Data Protection Regulation and the recitals yourself, click here: {% endblocktranslate %}
    https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN-DE/TXT/?from=EN&uri=CELEX%3A32016R0679
